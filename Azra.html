FAQs

Sunscreen helps protect your skin from harmful UV rays emitted by the sun. It prevents sunburn, premature aging, and reduces the risk of skin cancer.

Physical sunscreens contain mineral ingredients like zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, which reflect UV rays away from the skin. Chemical sunscreens, on the other hand, absorb UV rays and convert them into heat, which is then released from the skin

Natural ingredients like aloe vera, honey, and coconut oil have moisturizing and healing properties. For example, aloe vera can soothe sunburn, honey has antibacterial properties, and coconut oil is a great natural moisturizer.

Staying hydrated is essential for maintaining healthy skin. Proper hydration helps keep the skin moisturized, improves elasticity, and supports the skin's natural barrier function. Drinking enough water can also help reduce the appearance of wrinkles and promote a radiant complexion.

Exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells from the skin's surface, revealing smoother and brighter skin underneath. It can also unclog pores, prevent breakouts, and improve the effectiveness of other skincare products. However, it's important not to over-exfoliate, as it can cause irritation and damage to the skin.

Stress can have a negative impact on skin health by triggering various skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis. It can also accelerate the aging process by promoting the production of cortisol, which can lead to collagen breakdown and dullness in the skin.

One common skincare myth is that oily skin doesn't need moisturizer. In reality, even oily skin needs hydration to maintain a healthy moisture balance. Another myth is that natural skincare products are always better than synthetic ones. While natural ingredients can be beneficial, not all natural products are effective or safe for the skin.
